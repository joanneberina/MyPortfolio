The RV Driver: their needs and painpoints

After getting the big RV picture, we wanted to learn more about the RV community, their needs and pain points to solve the problem: How might we help RV drivers safely get from point A to point B? Understanding why this problem exists is the first step. So we ask the question:

How are the needs of a person driving an RV different from someone who drives a regular vehicle?

While doing surverys and user interviews, we learned that people driving an RV worry about things that wouldn’t normally be a concern for someone who drives a regular vehicle. We also wanted to know what features our users would like in a navigation app. These are the top responses from our users.

PAINPOINTS
  • Low clearances
  • Bumpy roads
  • Steep grades
  • Lack of on/off ramp warnings
  • Narrow city streets
  • Dangerous intersections
  • Not enough information about stops that accomodates RVs
  • Gas stations that do/don't have diesel
WANTS
  • Points of interest/li>
  • Parking lots
  • Offline usage
  • Clean showers/restroom locator
  • Gas stations that accomodate RVs
  • Live traffic update
  • Saving routes
  • Campground information
  • Avoiding tolls
  • Gas prices
  • Crowd-sourced information

The first solution is almost always not the right one

After doing sketches and exploring ideas, we came up with an initial mobile solution that had all the bells and whistles - it was like Google maps. It has an option to search for a point of interest as a destination, add stops along the route, featureing auto-complete and geo-location. And of course, we designed it to have a turn-by-turn voice navigation.

But this was not the right solution because we learned later on that we didn’t have the resources to get the right API that would provide the data for the points of interest. While all the initial features we designed were certainly “nice to haves”, it really did not directly impact the problem of safely routing the RV traveler from point A to point B.

Setting a priority based on user research

In a perfect world, providing all of these features and addressing all their pain points would be ideal. But we had to take into account the implementation, timeframe, and resources to come up with an effective solution, and at the same time set a reasonable goal for our product releases. Digging into our user insights...

User:

“The fastest route is not always the best route.”

Directions that are provided by other web mapping services is usually the fastest route which is oftentimes, not RV-friendly. The most common painpoint for people travelling in an RV is that they are afraid of getting into an accident. Safety is the number one concern for people traveling in RVs.
