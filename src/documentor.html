Overview

DocuMentor is an electronic visit verification (EVV) solution used to verify visit activity for home health, home care and hospice.

Electronic Visit Verification is a system for tracking work performed, hours, notes, accurate location information and much more about the patient-caregiver interaction, including information about the service provider. EVV applies to care and health services rendered in the home. The purpose of EVV is to ensure that services are delivered to people needing those services and that providers only bill for services rendered.

Target Users

Caregivers

Individuals who provide care and services to clients and carry out overall needs and plan of care. Caregivers can be family members or professional caregivers. Some services are also provided by Therapy Professionals, Behavioral Consultants, Music Therapists, Speech Therapists etc.

Provider Admins and Supervisors

They can be owners of an agency or the provider's employees who manages caregivers, assigns clients and/or set schedules for the caregivers. They make sure that hours used per client are within budget. They also review notes for each service and approves them for billing.

User Experience Audit

To be able to understand and relate to our customers and the team, it is essential to learn all about what EVV is and the industry that it serves. There were a lot of unfamiliar jargon and terminologies so talking to stakeholders, meeting with the developers and account managers was key to learning about how the app works and the current challenges that our customers were facing.

Both the CEO and the development team suggested that a UI redesign is much needed. When I was evaluating the current state of the app, it was apparent that the dashboard for both the caregiver and admin didn't have a clear information hierarchy. For example, there were several clickable icons but did not have the affordance that it is a link. It also did not have a text label that goes with it and that can be frustrating for first time users.

As the nature of EVV apps goes, this app also has quite a lot of form handling. And if you know about forms, they're not really fun to look at. The format looks a bit overwhelming and again, there's a lack of information hierarchy so it falls short of giving the user some guidance on where to start.

Most of the app's input forms are also in a modal dialogue. Although modal dialogs are very effective in attracting a user's attention to focus on the task at hand, it has more disadvantages in the way that it is utilized in the app. One downside is that the window goes away whenever the outside of the dialogue box is clicked and that can be disruptive because the user would have to open the modal again to finish the task. A link for viewing notes is also in a modal as well as many other interactions.

User Research Plan and Goals

It was clear that there were usability issues but they will remain as assumptions without verifying it from our users. Customer interviews are the most effective way to get direct feedback and validate our assumptions based from the UX audit. I was also looking forward to unique insights and discoveries that I may come across during this process.

But before diving into user interviews, it is essential to have a UX research plan which will help set expectations and document the essentials needed to communicate to stakeholders and customers. I outlined the general user interview goals and specific user interview goals for each target user.

General User Research Goals:

  • Understand how Caregivers and Admins are using the product.
  • Discover what the potential benefits of the product/experience to the users.
  • Learn about the clear or potential pain points.
  • Evaluate what features need improvement.

User Interview Goals

For Providers/Admin:

  • Learn how providers and admin utilize the dashboard. Find out what is useful and what is not.
  • Find out about how admins use the app to help in report generation for billing and reimbursement and specifically any challenges they may have in monitoring caregivers.
  • Learn about the scheduling process.

For Caregivers:

  • Learn about how and where they access and use the app.
  • Find out about any challenges in recording their hours, how and when they clock-in and clock-out.
  • Learn about how they utilize the notes section and what their level of engagement is.

For the Business Development Team:

  • Understand how each customer operates and what makes a customer sign-up for the product.
  • Gather feedback on how customers find value in the product.
  • Learn about customer onboarding process and their challenges when onboarding a customer.

Research Data Analysis

After doing remote interviews with 5 caregivers, 5 admins and 2 of our Business Development Managers, I put together my research data by writing down transcripts from the recorded interviews. And then organize it by doing affinity mapping. I organized the responses based on painpoints vs. needs and separated them according to user types. I then proceeded to further sort them by what feature or functionality the problem is about. By classifying information in this way, it was easier to identify problems and common issues. It was also easier to present to stakeholders because it is more digestible.

Notes and Hours, Reports and Forms, Budget Monitoring, Usability, Scheduling and Notifications are the areas where problems needed solving.

Feature Mapping - a collaborative activity

After presenting my analysis of the research data, I collaborated with our software development team to do a Feature Mapping. The goal of this collaboration was to get their feedback on what makes sense to prioritize according to the availability and limitation of time, tech stack and resources. It is going to be a long and iterative process given the number of problems that we wanted to solve. It was practical to tackle first what is already built and functioning so we decided to focus on fixing usability issues and redesigning the UI for the first feature release.
