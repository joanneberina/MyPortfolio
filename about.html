About Me...

My interest in design started as a child when I discovered my Aunt’s Architectural Digest Magazines and Ikea catalogs. My fascination with it led me to want to be an interior designer. Even though I loved design, I ended up taking Management Information Systems as my major. I liked computer programming and I was good at it.

For a decade, I worked in the marketing and advertising industry designing collaterals and learning about branding. I learned how to have an eye for detail and be mindful of visual hierarchy. I collaborated with marketing managers and constantly dealt with tight deadlines.

As a designer, I am both creative and analytical. I like designs that are engaging, simple and clean. Learning about the UX design process changed how I looked at Design. Some of my favorite books are “Sprint by Jake Knapp" and "Lean UX by Jeff Gothelf and Josh Sneiden."

In my free time, I like to garden and take care of my indoor plants. I am an adventurous eater who loves to cook. I especially like Asian food and anything spicy.

I am open to talking about UX or anything under the sun.
